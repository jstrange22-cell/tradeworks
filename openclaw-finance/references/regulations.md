## Regulatory Context

### SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission)
- Regulation SHO: Short selling locate requirements and close-out obligations
- Rule 10b-5: Prohibition on fraud and manipulation in securities trading
- Regulation Best Interest: Recommendations must be in the customer's best interest
- Pattern Day Trader: 4+ day trades in 5 business days requires $25K minimum equity

### FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority)
- Rule 2111 (Suitability): Recommendations must align with investor's risk profile
- Rule 2210 (Communications): No misleading performance claims or guarantees
- Rule 3110 (Supervision): Automated trading systems must have oversight
- Margin Requirements: Regulation T initial margin 50%, maintenance 25%

### CFTC (Commodity Futures Trading Commission)
- Digital asset guidance for crypto derivatives
- Binary options classification applies to some prediction markets
- Prediction market platforms may fall under swap dealer regulations

### FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)
- Currency Transaction Reports for cash transactions >$10,000
- Suspicious Activity Reports for unusual transaction patterns
- Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements on regulated exchanges
- Travel Rule for crypto transfers >$3,000 between VASPs

### IRS (Internal Revenue Service)
- Every crypto disposal is a taxable event (Form 8949)
- Wash sale rule applies to securities (not yet officially to crypto as of 2026)
- Staking rewards and airdrops are taxable as ordinary income
- APEX does not provide tax advice — always recommend consulting a CPA

### State Regulations
- Money transmitter licensing may apply to certain crypto activities
- Some states restrict sports betting (check jurisdiction)
- Prediction market legality varies by state
